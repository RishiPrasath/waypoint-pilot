# Malaysia Import Requirements

## Overview

Malaysia's import regulations are administered by the Royal Malaysian Customs Department (RMCD) under the Ministry of Finance. The country has streamlined trade processes through the uCustoms system and Malaysia National Single Window (NSW).

**Source**: U.S. International Trade Administration Country Commercial Guide (Last published: 2024-01-05)

## Key Regulatory Bodies

| Agency | Responsibility |
|--------|----------------|
| Royal Malaysian Customs Department (RMCD) | Customs clearance, duties |
| Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) | Trade policy, import licensing |
| Ministry of Domestic Trade and Cost of Living (KPDN) | Price control, consumer protection |
| SIRIM | Standards certification |
| JAKIM | Halal certification |

## Required Import Documentation

Exporting products to Malaysia requires:

1. **Invoice**
2. **Packing List**
3. **Delivery Letter**
4. **Leaflet, catalog or other related documents**
5. **Insurance Certificate**
6. **Bill of Lading/Airway Bill**
7. **Letter of Credit** (if applicable)
8. **Permit, licenses and certificates** (for regulated goods)
9. **Proof of fare payment**
10. **Declaration Form (Customs Form No. 1)**

### Customs Form No. 1

The declaration form must indicate:
- Number of packages/crates
- Description of goods
- Value
- Weight
- Quantity and type of goods
- Country of origin

### Submission Requirements

- Completed Customs forms should be submitted to Customs offices at the place where goods are imported
- All duties/customs taxes imposed on imported goods must be paid in advance before products can be released
- Additional documentation may be required to certify U.S./foreign content

## Import Licensing

### Non-Automatic Import Licensing

**17% of Malaysia's tariff lines** are subject to non-automatic import licensing, principally in:
- Construction equipment
- Agricultural sector
- Mineral sector
- Motor vehicle sector

This is designed to protect import-sensitive or strategic industries.

### Products Requiring Import License

- Motor vehicles
- Heavy machinery
- Certain agricultural products
- Rice and paddy
- Sugar
- Cement

## Prohibited and Restricted Imports

Quantitative import restrictions are seldom imposed except for:
- Protection of local industries
- Security reasons

For the full list of prohibited and restricted items, consult the Royal Malaysian Customs Department website (customs.gov.my).

### Prohibited Items

- Narcotics and psychotropic substances
- Counterfeit currency
- Pornographic materials
- Weapons without permit
- Goods bearing seditious inscriptions

### Restricted Items (Require Permit)

- Rice and paddy
- Sugar
- Cement
- Motor vehicles
- Poultry and eggs
- Timber products

## SIRIM Certification

### Malaysian Standards (MS)

Products must comply with relevant Malaysian Standards for safety and quality.

### Products Requiring SIRIM

- Electrical appliances
- Electronics
- Household products
- Building materials
- Automotive parts

## Halal Certification (JAKIM)

### JAKIM Certification

- Department of Islamic Development Malaysia
- Mandatory for food products marketed as Halal
- Internationally recognized

### Import Requirements

Products claiming Halal status must have:
- Valid Halal certificate from recognized foreign body, OR
- JAKIM certification upon entry

## Import Duties and Taxes

### Customs Valuation

Malaysia uses **CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight)** for customs valuation.

### Taxes to be Paid

| Tax Type | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Import Duty | 0% - 50% (varies by product) |
| Sales Tax | 5% or 10% |
| Excise Duty | Applies to specific goods |

### Low Value Goods (LVG) Tax

- Effective January 2024
- **10% tax** on imported goods valued MYR 500 or less
- Applies to goods purchased online from overseas sellers
- Registered overseas sellers collect and remit tax

## De Minimis Threshold

| Category | Threshold | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Duty-free threshold | MYR 500 | ~USD 107 |
| LVG tax applies | Above MYR 500 | 10% tax |

## Digital Systems

### uCustoms

Malaysia's customs management system featuring:
- Electronic submission of declarations
- Online payment of duties
- Real-time processing

### National Single Window (NSW)

- Single submission point for trade documents
- Integrated with multiple agencies
- Available at mytradelink.gov.my

## Free Zones

### Types of Free Zones

1. **Free Industrial Zones (FIZ)** - Manufacturing
2. **Free Commercial Zones (FCZ)** - Trading and warehousing

### Benefits

- Duty suspension
- Minimal customs formalities
- Simplified procedures

## Key Compliance Tips

1. **Verify import license requirements** before shipment
2. **Ensure correct HS classification** using Malaysian Tariff
3. **Obtain SIRIM certification** for regulated products
4. **Declare accurate values** - undervaluation attracts penalties
5. **Use uCustoms** for electronic declarations
6. **Check prohibited/restricted status** of goods
7. **Confirm documentary requirements** before shipping
