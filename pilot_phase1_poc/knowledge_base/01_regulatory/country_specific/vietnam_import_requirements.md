# Vietnam Import Requirements

## Overview

Vietnam's import regulations are managed by the General Department of Vietnam Customs (Tổng cục Hải quan) under the Ministry of Finance. The country has implemented the VNACCS/VCIS automated customs system.

**Source**: U.S. International Trade Administration Country Commercial Guide (Last published: 2024-01-30)

## Key Regulatory Bodies

| Agency | Responsibility |
|--------|----------------|
| General Department of Vietnam Customs | Customs clearance, duties |
| Ministry of Industry and Trade (MoIT) | Trade policy, import licensing |
| Ministry of Finance (MoF) | Tax policy, tariffs |
| Ministry of Health | Food safety, pharmaceuticals |
| Ministry of Agriculture | Agricultural products, quarantine |

## Authorized Importers

### Vietnamese Traders

Vietnamese traders are entitled to:
- Export goods of all kinds (except goods banned from export)
- Import goods according to business lines stated in business registration certificates

### Foreign-Invested Enterprises

Foreign-invested enterprises and business cooperation parties:
- May export goods of other kinds (except banned/restricted categories)
- Imports must comply with granted investment licenses
- Must follow the Law on Foreign Investment in Vietnam

## Import Licensing System

### Business Entities

Business entities with a legally registered business license may engage in direct import and export activities.

### Foreign Invested Enterprises

- Can import materials, equipment, and machinery for establishing production lines
- Must be in accordance with investment licenses
- Under Vietnam's WTO commitments, trading rights are open to all foreign invested enterprises

### Automatic Import Licensing

Vietnam facilitates an automatic import licensing system requiring importers of wide categories of goods to obtain a license from MoIT for customs clearance.

### Distribution Rights

- Open to joint venture investment (no limit on capital contribution)
- Since 2009, opened to wholly foreign invested enterprises

## Special Import Requirements

### Seven Ministries Oversight

Seven ministries and agencies oversee minimum quality/performance standards for:
- Animal and plant protection
- Health safety
- Local network compatibility (telecommunications)
- Money security
- Cultural sensitivity

Goods meeting minimum standards can be imported upon demand in unlimited quantity and value.

## Required Import Documentation

### Standard Documents

1. **Customs Declaration** (electronic submission)
2. **Bill of Lading/Airway Bill**
3. **Commercial Invoice**
4. **Packing List**
5. **Certificate of Origin** (for preferential rates)
6. **Import License** (if applicable)

### Additional Documents (Product-Specific)

- Quality inspection certificate
- Quarantine certificate (agricultural products)
- Health registration (food, cosmetics)
- Conformity certificate (regulated products)

## Labeling Requirements

### Mandatory Labeling

Goods must have labels with:
- **Origin** (country of manufacture)
- **Product name**
- **Responsible entity** (importer/distributor)
- **Expiry date** (where applicable)
- **Vietnamese language** (for consumer goods)

### Supplementary Labels

- Foreign language labels acceptable if Vietnamese supplement attached
- Must be affixed before customs clearance

## Electronic Certificate of Origin (e-C/O)

### Requirements

- e-C/O must be **color-printed on A4 paper**
- **QR codes required** for verification
- Supported forms: E, AI, AJ, AANZ, AHK, RCEP, S, VC, VJ, VN-CU, CPTPP

## Declaration Timeline

| Requirement | Timeline |
|-------------|----------|
| Customs declaration | Within **15 days** of vessel arrival |
| Payment of duties | Before goods release |
| Document retention | **5 years** after clearance |

## Import Duties and Taxes

### Customs Valuation

Vietnam uses **CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight)** for customs valuation.

### Tax Rates

| Tax Type | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Import Duty (MFN) | 0% - 135% |
| VAT (Standard) | 10% |
| VAT (Reduced) | 5% (essential goods) |
| Special Consumption Tax | Varies (luxury goods) |

### Preferential Rates Available Under

- ATIGA (ASEAN)
- RCEP
- EVFTA (EU-Vietnam FTA)
- CPTPP
- Bilateral FTAs

## Key Regulatory Changes (2024/2025)

### Tariff Decrees

- **Decree 144/2024/ND-CP** (November 2024): Amended preferential import tariffs
- Alignment of CPTPP tariffs with existing decrees

### AEO Program

- Vietnam signed **AEO Mutual Recognition Agreement** with Korea (late 2024)
- Benefits for trusted traders
- Expedited customs clearance

## Prohibited and Restricted Imports

### Prohibited Items

- Narcotics
- Weapons (without permit)
- Counterfeit goods
- Pornographic materials
- Toxic chemicals

### Restricted Items (Require Permit)

- Cultural products
- Used goods (certain categories)
- Right-hand drive vehicles
- Sensitive technology

## Digital Customs System

### VNACCS/VCIS

Vietnam Automated Cargo Clearance System features:
- Electronic declaration submission
- 24/7 processing capability
- Risk-based inspection
- Electronic payment
- Real-time tracking

## Key Compliance Tips

1. **Use correct e-C/O format** with QR codes
2. **Apply Vietnamese labels** before clearance
3. **File declarations within 15 days**
4. **Maintain 5-year records**
5. **Verify preferential tariff eligibility**
6. **Register for AEO benefits** if eligible
7. **Comply with quarantine, food safety, and quality standards**
