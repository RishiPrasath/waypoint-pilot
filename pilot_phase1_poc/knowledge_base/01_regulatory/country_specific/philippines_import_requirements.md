# Philippines Import Requirements

## Overview

The Philippines import regulations are managed by the Bureau of Customs (BOC) under the Department of Finance. The Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) oversees trade policy and import services.

**Source**: U.S. International Trade Administration Country Commercial Guide (Last published: 2024-01-24)

## Key Regulatory Bodies

| Agency | Responsibility |
|--------|----------------|
| Bureau of Customs (BOC) | Customs clearance, duties |
| Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) | Trade policy, import services |
| Bureau of Import Services (BIS) | Import classifications, procedures |
| Food and Drug Administration (FDA) | Food, drugs, cosmetics |
| Department of Agriculture | Agricultural products, MAV certificates |

## Import Classifications

Imports are classified as:

| Classification | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Freely Importable** | No restrictions |
| **Regulated** | Requires clearances/licenses prior to importation |
| **Restricted** | Subject to special requirements |
| **Prohibited** | Not allowed |

### Verification Resources

- **BOC-CARES**: boc.cares@customs.gov.ph, Tel: (632) 8705-6000
- **Bureau of Import Services (BIS)**: bis@dti.gov.ph
- **Department of Agriculture**: For agricultural products, MAV certificates

## Required Import Documentation

### Standard Documents

1. **Duly endorsed Bill of Lading or Airway Bill** (or carrier certification)
2. **Commercial Invoice**
3. **Letter of Credit** (or other verifiable commercial document evidencing payment)
4. **Packing List**
5. **Duly notarized Supplemental Declaration on Valuation (SDV)**

### Additional Documents (As Required)

- Import Permit or Clearance
- Authority to Release Imported Goods (ATRIG)
- Proof of Origin for Free Trade Agreements (FTAs)
- Copy of Advance Ruling (if used in goods declaration)
- Load Port Survey Reports or Discharge Port Survey Reports (for bulk/break-bulk)
- Document evidencing exemption from duties and taxes
- Tax Credit Certificate (TCC) or Tax Debit Memo (TDM)

## Entry Types

### Formal Entry

- For commercial goods valued at **PHP 50,000+** (FOB/FCA)
- Full documentation required
- Standard customs processing

### Informal Entry

- For goods valued **below PHP 50,000**
- Simplified procedures
- Faster clearance

## Importer Registration

### Requirements for New Importers

1. Register with BOC
2. Provide valid identification documents
3. Police/NBI Clearance
4. Business Registration (DTI or SEC)
5. Company Profile

## De Minimis Threshold

| Category | Threshold | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Duty-free personal goods | PHP 10,000 | ~USD 170 |
| Excludes | Restricted items | Alcohol, tobacco, etc. |

## Regulated Commodities

Regulated commodities can be imported only after securing:
- Necessary goods declaration
- Clearances
- Licenses
- Other requirements **prior to importation**

Submission of requirements after arrival (but prior to release from BOC custody) is allowed only in cases provided by governing laws or regulations.

## Letter of Credit Transactions

### L/C Transaction Requirements

- Duly accomplished L/C
- Pro-Forma Invoice
- Single Administrative Document (SAD) for Advance Customs Import Duty

### Non-L/C Transactions

- Pro-Forma Invoice required for:
  - Draft Documents against Acceptance (D/A)
  - Documents against Payment (D/P)
  - Open Account (OA)
  - Self-funded documentation

## Submission Process

Documents must be submitted to:
- **Bureau of Customs' Formal Entry Division (FED)** or equivalent office
- Include printout of SAD signed by declarant and customs broker (if any)
- Must be notarized

## Import Duties and Taxes

### Customs Valuation

Philippines uses **CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight)** for customs valuation.

### Tax Rates

| Tax Type | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Import Duty (MFN) | 0% - 65% |
| VAT | 12% |
| Excise Tax | Varies (alcohol, tobacco, petroleum, automobiles) |

### Calculation

```
Import Duty = CIF Value × Duty Rate
VAT = (CIF Value + Duty + Excise Tax) × 12%
```

## FDA-Regulated Products

### Products Under FDA Control

- Food products
- Drugs and medicines
- Medical devices
- Cosmetics
- Household hazardous substances

### Process

1. FDA Registration as importer
2. Product notification/registration
3. Import permit
4. Clearance at customs

## Agricultural Products

### MAV (Minimum Access Volume) Import Certificate

Required for certain agricultural products:
- Swine
- Chicken
- Other MAV-covered products

Contact USDA Foreign Agricultural Service Manila (AgManila@usda.gov) for MAV information.

## Key Compliance Tips

1. **Verify import classification** with BOC-CARES or BIS before shipping
2. **Complete SDV accurately** - mandatory and notarized
3. **Register with BOC** before importing
4. **Obtain product-specific permits** early
5. **File proper declarations** through e-customs system
6. **Verify FTA eligibility** for preferential rates
7. **Work with licensed customs broker** for compliance
