# Thailand Import Requirements

## Overview

Thailand's import regulations are administered by the Thai Customs Department under the Ministry of Finance. The country has implemented the Thailand National Single Window (NSW) for electronic trade documentation.

**Source**: U.S. International Trade Administration Country Commercial Guide (Last published: 2024-01-09)

## Key Regulatory Bodies

| Agency | Responsibility |
|--------|----------------|
| Thai Customs Department | Customs clearance, duties |
| Ministry of Commerce | Import licensing, trade policy |
| Food and Drug Administration (FDA) | Food, drugs, cosmetics |
| Thai Industrial Standards Institute (TISI) | Product standards |
| Department of Foreign Trade | Trade regulations |

## Import Licenses

Import licenses are required for the importation of:
- Raw materials
- Petroleum
- Industrial machinery
- Textiles
- Pharmaceuticals
- Firearms and ammunition
- Agricultural items

Some imports not requiring licenses are subject to:
- Extra fees
- Certificate of origin requirements

## Required Import Documentation

### Standard Documents

1. **Customs Import Entry Form**
2. **Commercial Invoice**
3. **Packing List**
4. **Bill of Lading/Airway Bill**
5. **Letter of Credit** (if applicable)
6. **Import/Export License** (for regulated goods)
7. **Authorization from relevant agencies** (if required)

### Products Requiring Additional Authorization

| Product Category | Authorizing Agency |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Processed foods, pharmaceuticals, vitamins, cosmetics | Food and Drug Administration, Ministry of Public Health |
| Tungsten oxide, tin ores, metallic tin (>2kg) | Department of Mineral Resources, Ministry of Industry |
| Arms, ammunition, explosive devices | Ministry of Interior |
| Antiques or objects of art | Fine Arts Department, Ministry of Education |

## Import Controls

### Products Subject to Control

- Food products (processed or unprocessed)
- Pharmaceuticals
- Medical devices
- Healthcare products
- Cosmetics
- Hazardous substances
- Animals
- Agricultural products

### Tariff-Rate Quotas (TRQs)

Thailand imposes domestic purchase requirements on importers of:
- Soybeans and soybean meal
- Feed wheat

### WTO Commitments

Thailand has eliminated its requirement of certificate of origin for information technology imports pursuant to the WTO Information Technology Agreement.

## De Minimis Threshold

| Category | Threshold | Status |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Duty exemption | THB 1,500 | ~USD 42 |
| Effective until | January 1, 2026 | **Ending** |

### Important Change (January 2026)

From **January 1, 2026**, ALL imported goods will be subject to duties regardless of value. The de minimis exemption is being eliminated.

### VAT Requirements

- **Standard VAT**: 7%
- Applies to most imported goods
- VAT applies to goods valued above THB 1

## TISI Certification

### Thai Industrial Standards Institute

Products requiring TIS (Thai Industrial Standard) certification:
- Electrical appliances
- Steel products
- Construction materials
- Automotive parts
- Toys

## FDA Requirements

### Products Under FDA Control

- Food products
- Pharmaceuticals
- Medical devices
- Cosmetics
- Hazardous substances

### Import Process for FDA Products

1. Register as FDA importer
2. Apply for product notification/registration
3. Obtain import license
4. Clear customs with FDA approval

## Import Duties and Taxes

### Customs Valuation

Thailand uses **CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight)** for customs valuation.

### Tax Rates

| Tax Type | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Import Duty | 0% - 80% (varies by product) |
| VAT | 7% |
| Excise Tax | Varies (alcohol, tobacco, vehicles, petroleum) |

### Calculation

```
Import Duty = CIF Value × Duty Rate
VAT = (CIF Value + Import Duty + Excise Tax) × 7%
```

## E-Customs System

### Thailand National Single Window (NSW)

Features:
- Electronic submission of trade documents
- Integration with multiple agencies
- Real-time processing
- 24/7 availability

### Benefits

- Online declaration submission
- Electronic payment
- Track shipment status

## Free Zones

### Types

1. **Free Trade Zone (FTZ)** - Duty-free storage and processing
2. **Export Processing Zone (EPZ)** - Manufacturing for export
3. **Special Economic Zone (SEZ)** - Border economic areas

### Benefits

- Duty exemption/deferral
- Simplified procedures
- Reduced documentation

## Key Compliance Tips

1. **Prepare for de minimis change** (January 2026)
2. **Verify FDA requirements** for controlled products
3. **Check TIS certification needs** for regulated goods
4. **Use correct HS codes** - Thailand uses AHTN
5. **Register with e-Customs** for smooth clearance
6. **Verify FTA eligibility** for preferential rates
7. **Use a freight forwarder** recommended for customs clearing

## ASEAN Integration

Thailand participates in:
- **ATIGA**: 0% tariff with Form D for most goods
- **ASW**: Electronic Certificate of Origin
- **AWSC**: Self-certification for certified exporters
- **ACTS**: Customs transit system
