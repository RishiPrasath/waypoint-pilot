## 1. Purpose

This procedure establishes the process for handling Collect on Delivery (COD) shipments where freight and charges are collected from the consignee at destination rather than prepaid by the shipper.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to all shipments where:
- Freight charges are payable at destination
- Third-party charges require collection
- Import duties/taxes collected on behalf of customer

---

## 3. COD Types

| COD Type | Description | Risk Level |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Freight Collect** | Ocean/air freight payable by consignee | Medium |
| **Charges Collect** | All charges (freight + local) payable by consignee | High |
| **Duty Collect** | Duties/taxes collected from consignee | Medium |
| **Full COD** | All charges including duties payable at delivery | High |

---

## 4. Eligibility and Approval

### 4.1 Customer Eligibility

| Criteria | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Account status | Active, no overdue invoices |
| Credit history | Minimum 6 months good payment history |
| COD limit | Within approved credit limit |
| Consignee verification | Known consignee with payment capability |

### 4.2 Approval Matrix

| COD Amount | Approval Level |
|------------|----------------|
| < SGD 5,000 | Team Lead |
| SGD 5,000 - 20,000 | Manager |
| SGD 20,000 - 50,000 | Director |
| > SGD 50,000 | MD approval |

### 4.3 Restricted COD

COD not permitted for:
- New customers (< 3 months)
- Customers with payment defaults
- High-risk destinations
- Perishable/time-sensitive cargo
- First shipment to unknown consignee

---

## 5. COD Booking Process

### Step 1: COD Request

**Customer Service Actions:**

1. Receive COD request from shipper
2. Verify shipper account eligibility
3. Collect consignee information:
   - Full legal name
   - Address
   - Contact person and phone
   - Email
   - Business registration (if company)

4. Calculate total COD amount:
   | Component | Source |
   |-----------|--------|
   | Freight | Carrier rate |
   | Origin charges | Internal charges |
   | Destination charges | Agent quote |
   | Duties/taxes | Estimated |
   | Handling fee | Per tariff |

### Step 2: Credit Assessment

**Finance Team Actions:**

1. Verify shipper credit status
2. Check outstanding COD receivables
3. Assess consignee (if known):
   - Previous payment history
   - Business verification
   - Country risk

4. Recommend approval/rejection

### Step 3: COD Approval

**Approving Authority Actions:**

1. Review credit assessment
2. Consider risk factors
3. Approve with conditions or reject
4. Document approval in system

**Approval Conditions May Include:**
- Reduced COD limit
- Advance deposit required
- Bank guarantee
- Partial prepayment

## 6. Documentation Requirements

### 6.1 Shipper Documentation

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| COD authorization form | Shipper's written instruction |
| Letter of undertaking | Shipper guarantees payment if consignee defaults |
| Consignee details | Contact and billing information |

### 6.2 Transport Documentation

| Document | Notation Required |
|----------|-------------------|
| Bill of Lading | "FREIGHT COLLECT" prominently marked |
| Air Waybill | "CHARGES COLLECT" in charges box |
| Commercial invoice | COD amount stated |

### 6.3 Agent Instructions

Pre-alert to destination agent must include:
- COD amount and breakdown
- Acceptable payment methods
- Collection deadline
- Release conditions
- Escalation contacts

---

## 7. Collection Process

### 7.1 Pre-Arrival

**Operations Actions:**

1. Send pre-alert to destination agent (5 days before ETA)
2. Include COD collection instructions:
   ```
   COD COLLECTION INSTRUCTIONS

   B/L Number: XXXX
   Consignee: [Name]

   Total COD Amount: USD XXXX
   Breakdown:
   - Ocean freight: USD XXX
   - Destination charges: USD XXX
   - Duties (estimated): USD XXX

   Collection Deadline: Before cargo release

   Acceptable Payment:
   - Bank transfer (preferred)
   - Certified check
   - Cash (with approval)

   DO NOT RELEASE CARGO UNTIL PAYMENT CONFIRMED
   ```

3. Notify consignee of arrival and COD amount

### 7.2 At Destination

**Agent Actions:**

1. Contact consignee upon arrival
2. Provide final COD amount (with actual duties)
3. Collect payment before release
4. Verify payment cleared (if check/transfer)
5. Release cargo only after confirmation
6. Send proof of payment to origin

### 7.3 Payment Verification

| Payment Method | Verification Required |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Bank transfer | Bank confirmation |
| Certified check | Bank clearance (2-3 days) |
| Cash | Receipt and deposit confirmation |
| Letter of credit | Bank advice |

## 8. Fund Remittance

### 8.1 Remittance Timeline

| From Agent to Company | Timeline |
|----------------------|----------|
| Payment confirmation | Within 24 hours of receipt |
| Fund remittance | Within 7 days of receipt |
| Supporting documents | With remittance |

### 8.2 Remittance Instructions

Agent remits to:
- Company bank account (TT)
- Net of agreed commission
- With detailed breakdown

**Remittance Advice Must Include:**
- B/L/AWB numbers
- Consignee names
- Amounts collected
- Deductions (if any)
- Exchange rate applied

---

## 9. Exception Handling

### 9.1 Non-Payment Scenarios

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Consignee refuses payment | Hold cargo, notify shipper immediately |
| Consignee disputes amount | Verify with shipper, resolve before release |
| Consignee requests credit | Not permitted without prior approval |
| Consignee cannot be reached | Hold cargo, escalate to shipper |

### 9.2 Disputed COD

**Process:**

1. Do NOT release cargo
2. Document dispute details
3. Notify shipper within 24 hours
4. Request shipper instructions:
   - Confirm original amount
   - Authorize adjustment
   - Arrange cargo return
5. Obtain written approval for any changes

### 9.3 Uncollected COD

If cargo not collected within 14 days:

1. Issue abandonment notice to consignee
2. Notify shipper of situation
3. Request shipper instructions:
   - Extend free time (at shipper's cost)
   - Return cargo to origin
   - Dispose/auction cargo
4. Shipper liable for all accrued charges

---

## 10. Shipper Liability

### 10.1 Shipper Guarantee

By requesting COD terms, shipper agrees:

- Guarantee payment if consignee defaults
- Pay all charges if cargo returned
- Cover storage and demurrage
- Indemnify company against losses

### 10.2 Recovery from Shipper

If consignee fails to pay:

| Timeline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Day 1 | Notify shipper of non-payment |
| Day 7 | Formal demand to shipper |
| Day 14 | Invoice shipper for full amount |
| Day 30 | Suspend shipper account |
| Day 60 | Collection/legal action |

## 11. COD Fees

| Service | Fee |
|---------|-----|
| COD handling fee | SGD 50 or 1% of COD (whichever higher) |
| COD amendment | SGD 25 |
| Non-payment handling | SGD 100 |
| Cargo return processing | Actual cost + 15% |

## 12. Reporting

### 12.1 COD Aging Report

| Aging Bucket | Review Frequency |
|--------------|------------------|
| Current (0-30 days) | Weekly |
| 31-60 days | Twice weekly |
| 61-90 days | Daily |
| > 90 days | Escalate to Director |

### 12.2 Monthly COD Summary

Report includes:
- Total COD shipments
- Total COD value
- Collection rate
- Outstanding COD by age
- Problem accounts

---

## 13. Controls and Audit

### 13.1 Segregation of Duties

| Role | Cannot Also |
|------|-------------|
| COD approver | Release cargo |
| Cargo release | Confirm payment |
| Payment reconciler | Approve COD |

### 13.2 Reconciliation

- Daily: Agent remittance vs. collection
- Weekly: Outstanding COD review
- Monthly: Full COD reconciliation
- Quarterly: Agent account audit
