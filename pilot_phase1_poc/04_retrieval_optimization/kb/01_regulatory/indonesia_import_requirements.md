# Indonesia Import Requirements

## Key Terms and Abbreviations

| Abbreviation | Full Term | Context |
|:------------|:----------|:--------|
| **INSW** | Indonesia National Single Window | Online portal for import/export permits and declarations |
| **LARTAS** | Larangan dan Pembatasan | Restricted and prohibited goods requiring special permits |
| **SNI** | Standar Nasional Indonesia | Mandatory product certification for 130+ categories |
| **API** | Angka Pengenal Importir | Importer Identification Number (required for all imports) |
| **PIB** | Pemberitahuan Impor Barang | Import Declaration Form submitted to Bea Cukai |
| **BPOM** | Badan Pengawas Obat dan Makanan | Food & drug regulatory agency (approvals for F&B, cosmetics) |
| **OSS** | Online Single Submission | Business licensing system integrated with INSW |
| **NIB** | Nomor Induk Berusaha | Business Identification Number (replaces older licenses) |

## Overview

Indonesia's import regulations are managed by the Directorate General of Customs and Excise (DGCE/Bea Cukai) under the Ministry of Finance. The country has implemented the Online Single Submission (OSS) system and Indonesia National Single Window (INSW) to streamline import processes.

**Source**: U.S. International Trade Administration Country Commercial Guide (Last published: 2025-11-17)

## Key Regulatory Bodies

| Agency | Indonesian Name | Responsibility |

| Directorate General of Customs and Excise | Bea Cukai | Customs clearance, duties |
| Ministry of Trade | Kemendag | Import licensing, trade policy |

| Ministry of Agriculture | Kementan | Agricultural products, quarantine |

## Required Import Documentation

At a minimum, import documentation must include:

1. **Proforma Invoice**
2. **Commercial Invoice**
3. **Certificate of Origin** (for preferential tariff claims)
4. **Bill of Lading or Airway Bill**
5. **Packing List**
6. **Insurance Certificate**

### Electronic Submission

Documents must be submitted electronically through the **Indonesia National Single Window (INSW)** platform using standardized Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) formats. Submissions typically occur prior to cargo arrival.

## Import Identification Number (API)

All importers must hold a valid **Import Identification Number (API)** and the appropriate import license.

### Business Identification Number (NIB)

Under the Omnibus Law on Job Creation and Government Regulation No. 29 of 2021, various import licenses have been consolidated into a single **Business Identification Number (Nomor Induk Berusaha or NIB)**, obtained through Indonesia's Online Single Submission (OSS) system.

The NIB now serves as a unified substitute for:
- **API-U** (General Import License)
- **API-P** (Producer Import License)
- **API-T** (Limited Import License)

## LARTAS (Prohibited & Restricted Goods)

LARTAS = **Larangan dan Pembatasan** (Prohibitions and Restrictions)

The importation of specific goods is strictly regulated under the **Prohibited and Restricted Goods (LARTAS) list**, as specified in **Ministry of Trade Regulation No. 18 of 2021**.

### Checking LARTAS Status

Importers are required to consult the **INSW portal** (eservice.insw.go.id) to identify any applicable restrictions or prohibitions for their goods.

### Regulatory Agencies for LARTAS

Technical regulations are governed by:

| Ministry/Agency | Scope |
|-----------------|-------|
| Minister of Trade | Consumer goods, chemicals, electronics |
| Minister of Industry | Industrial goods, technology, electronic products |
| Minister of Agriculture | Animals, plants, derivative products |
| Minister of Environment and Forestry | Hazardous/toxic waste materials |
| National Agency of Drug and Food Control (BPOM) | Pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, food products |
| National Police | Firearms |

### Prohibited Items (Per Ministry of Trade Regulation 18/2021)

- **Sugar**: Raw Crystal Sugar, Refined Crystal Sugar, White Crystal Sugar with certain ICUMSA values
- **Rice**: Rice with broken grain content 5-25%, including sticky rice, Hom Mali, Basmati
- **Ozone-Depleting Substances**: CFCs, HCFCs, halons, carbon tetrachloride
- **Used Items**: Used bags, sacks, and clothing (particularly jute/textile fibers)
- **Cooling Equipment**: Refrigerators/freezers using CFC or HCFC-22
- **Hazardous Materials (B3)**: DDT, Aldrin, PCBs, hazardous slag/ash
- **Finished Hand Tools**: Agricultural tools (shovels, hoes, rakes) except listed exemptions
- **Mercury Medical Devices**: Dental amalgam, BP devices, thermometers with mercury

**Penalties**: Violations may result in imprisonment up to 5 years and/or fine up to IDR 5 billion.

## SNI (Indonesian National Standard)

**Standar Nasional Indonesia** is the mandatory technical standard for certain products.

### Products Requiring SNI

- Electronics and electrical appliances
- Automotive parts
- Steel products
- Toys
- Construction materials
- Food products

## Halal Certification

For some products, such as food and cosmetics, **Halal certification** is mandatory.

### Certification Authority
- **BPJPH** (Badan Penyelenggara Jaminan Produk Halal)

## Import Duties and Taxes

### Customs Valuation
Indonesia uses **CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight)** for customs valuation.

### Taxes Applicable
- **Import Duty**: Based on HS code classification
- **VAT (PPN)**: 12% (effective January 1, 2025 per Law No. 7/2021 and PMK 131/2024)
  - *Note*: For non-luxury goods, the effective rate remains 11% through a DPP calculation formula (12% Ã— 11/12)
  - Luxury goods (subject to PPnBM) pay full 12% VAT
- **Luxury Goods Tax (PPnBM)**: 10% - 200% (for luxury items)
- **Income Tax (PPh 22)**: 2.5% - 10% (prepayment)

## De Minimis Threshold

| Category | Threshold | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Personal shipments | USD 3 | Very low threshold |
| Commercial shipments | No de minimis | All subject to duties |

## Key Compliance Tips

1. **Obtain NIB** through OSS system before importing
2. **Check LARTAS status** on INSW portal for every HS code
3. **Verify SNI requirements** for regulated products
4. **Ensure Halal certification** for food/cosmetics
5. **Use correct HS classification** - determines duties and LARTAS status
6. **Work with licensed customs broker** familiar with INSW system
